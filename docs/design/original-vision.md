# Lanternkeeper: Emberfall — Original Vision Document

*Written by Allie*

## What this project is

Lanternkeeper: Emberfall is a small mobile game I'm building as a gift and support tool for Garrett, who is a solo indie game developer. It's designed to help him feel less alone, less pressured, and more grounded while working on his own game (Atlas).

At its core, this isn't a productivity app or a habit tracker.
It's a ritual-based companion game that reframes creative work as something gentle, iterative, and shared.

The player isn't being managed.
They're building alongside someone.

## The core fantasy

The player meets a small red panda character named Aetherling, who is also a developer.

- Aetherling is building a quiet world called Emberfall
- The player is building their own creative project (Atlas)
- They don't rush each other
- They compare notes, not output

This mirrors the philosophy you've always emphasized:
learning and building are collaborative, messy, and allowed to be imperfect.

## What the game actually looks like

The game opens to a single, calm scene called Lantern Clearing.

There's:
- A foggy forest clearing
- A lantern
- Aetherling sitting nearby
- Subtle fog and leaves on the ground

Nothing is loud. Nothing is urgent.

Instead of asking "What do you need to do today?"
The game asks:

"Could you help me clear the way?"

## The Helping Hands mechanic (the heart of the game)

Before any reflection or task appears, the player does something physical but tiny:
- Swipe away fog
- Brush leaves off the ground

This takes a few seconds.
It's intentionally low-effort and tactile.

### Why this matters

This is behavioral activation without shame.

It embodies the idea that:
- Movement comes before clarity
- You don't need to understand everything to begin
- Small actions are valid actions

The player isn't "logging a mood."
They're helping someone else.
And in the process, they help themselves start.

## Emotional check-in (without therapy language)

After helping clear the scene, the player is gently asked:

How are you feeling?
- Stuck
- Frustrated
- Inspired
- Alright

These aren't diagnoses.
They're simple, human states.

The game doesn't react with advice or analysis.
It just adjusts the tone of what comes next.

## Micro-quests (ramps, not assignments)

Based on the chosen feeling, the game offers one tiny suggestion, such as:
- "Open your project for 2 minutes"
- "Write one sentence about what feels blocked"
- "Protect your spark: remove one distraction"

Only one appears at a time.
No lists. No streaks. No penalties.

This is directly inspired by your teaching style:
build ramps, not hurdles.

## Aetherling's role (this is important)

Aetherling is not:
- A pet that depends on you
- A cheerleader
- A therapist
- A productivity coach

Aetherling is:
- A peer
- A quiet collaborator
- A fellow builder

He never says "good job."
He never says "you should."
He never expresses disappointment.

He assumes effort and goodwill by default.

That tone is intentional and foundational.

## The lanterns and the regions of Emberfall

As the player returns over time, small parts of Emberfall unlock.
These are not levels — they are emotional spaces.

There are five regions:

1. **Lantern Clearing** (Home) — Daily return, grounding ritual.
2. **Workshop Glade** — A quiet desk for ideas and creative notes.
3. **Fog Valley** — A place that reframes being stuck as information, not failure.
4. **Warm River** — A space for frustration to dissolve without fixing.
5. **Observatory Balcony** — A rare place to reflect on meaning: "Why does this project matter to you?"

The world stays small on purpose.
There is no map screen.
No sense of "falling behind."

You fade into places, then return home.

## The reset mechanic ("Begin again")

One of the most important features is the ability to reset the scene.

There's a quiet option to:
- Relight the lantern
- Let fog and leaves return
- Begin the morning again

This does not erase progress.
It doesn't scold or warn.

It exists to embody this belief:

"Nothing broke. We're just choosing a quieter beginning."

This idea is directly inspired by how you've always approached learning and iteration.

## What I'm trying to build emotionally

When someone opens this app, I want them to feel:
- understood
- inspired
- less pressured

Not motivated.
Not optimized.

Just companioned.
